About

Sergio Perez has accumulated more points than any other Mexican in the history of Formula 1. He has stood on the podium 28 times and has had 5 first place finishes. Perez joined F1 with Sauber in 2011 and became the fifth Mexican to compete in Formula 1 and the first since Hectoré Rebaque who competed from 1977-1981. The first race Perez took part in with F1 he finished in the points with a 7th place finish and was the only driver that race to make fewer than two stops.

Perez was a member of the Ferrari Driver Academy up until 2012 when he signed with McLaren for the 2013 season. Unfortunately, the team did not score a single point the whole season and all of the successes he had in his F1 debut did not help him. His seat at McLaren was replaced. Perez signed a contract with Force India in 2014 and raced with the team until they went into administration in 2018 and turned into Racing Point in 2019. In 2020 his seat was replaced by Sebastian Vettel and was not a driver for F1 until he signed with Red Bull Racing for the 2021 season.

Sergio Perez was born in Mexico and is the youngest child of Antonio Perezé Garibay and Mariluù Mendoza de Perez. His older brother, Antonio, is a retired stock car driver who competed in the NASCAR Mexico Series. In an interview in 2012 he stated if he wasn't a F1 driver he would have liked to have been a lawyer.

Early Career

Karting

Starting his karting days in 1996 at the age of six, he claimed four victories in the junior category and claimed the runner-up spot in the category. In 1998, he became the youngest driver to become the champion of the Youth Class in karting with 8 wins.

Formula 3 and GP2 Series

Perez joined the British Formula 3 Championship for the 2007 season. He won my a comfortable margin and won two-thirds of the races and pole positions. He finished all but two races on the podium. In 2008 he graduated to the premier International Class of the championship. Perez had an early lead in the championship by gaining a large lead early on but eventually went on to finish fourth in the drivers' standings.

For GP2, Perez drove for the Campos Grand Prix team. He was the first Mexican driver to compete at this level of motorsport since 1990. He won the first GP2 Asia Series at Sakhir and had a second win at Losail. 2009 lead Perez to move to Arden International and he finished 12th in the standings.
